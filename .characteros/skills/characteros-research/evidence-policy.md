# CharacterOS Research Evidence Policy

## Claim Labels

Use exactly these epistemic labels:

- `ESTABLISHED_EVIDENCE`: supported by converging high-quality primary evidence for the stated scope.
- `PLAUSIBLE_HYPOTHESIS`: compatible with evidence but not sufficiently validated for CharacterOS.
- `ENGINEERING_HEURISTIC`: chosen for bounded system behavior, not claimed as psychological truth.
- `UNSUPPORTED`: insufficient, contradictory, or non-transferable evidence.

Never collapse these labels into a generic statement that a model is scientifically proven.

## Preferred Sources

Prioritize:

1. peer-reviewed primary research;
2. systematic reviews and meta-analyses with inspectable methods;
3. original validated scale or measurement papers;
4. longitudinal and intensive-longitudinal datasets;
5. official standards and library documentation for technical claims.

Use preprints cautiously and label them. Do not use blogs, marketing pages, generated summaries, or unsourced diagrams as scientific authority.

## Citation Discipline

- Cite the source next to the claim it supports.
- State whether a claim is direct evidence or an inference.
- Do not quote a result without its population and timescale when those affect transferability.
- Do not invent effect sizes, confidence intervals, coefficients, or causal direction.
- Record conflicting evidence and negative findings.

## Clinical And Ethical Boundary

CharacterOS must not claim diagnosis, treatment guidance, clinical prediction, or validated assessment. Research about clinical populations may inform a bounded simulation hypothesis only after transfer limits are explicit.

Avoid deterministic labels about a person. Prefer statements about simulated state, uncertainty, and model assumptions.

## Parameter Policy

A paper may justify:

- adding a state distinction;
- choosing a qualitative direction;
- defining a plausible timescale range;
- designing a calibration target;
- adding an audit or falsification case.

A paper alone does not justify a production coefficient. Coefficients require project-specific calibration, sensitivity analysis, trajectory tests, and versioned rollout evidence.

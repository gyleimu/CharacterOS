## Objective

Establish the dream fragment system — minimal, non-narrative dream generation during sleep. Dreams are psychological residue: memory fragments, unmet needs, stress, and belief tension surfacing during sleep phases.

This is NOT a dream story generator. No LLM. No narrative prose.

---

## Why Dream Is Fragment, Not Story

CharacterOS dreams are not authored narratives. They are:

- **Residue** — what remains after a day of experiences
- **Signal** — emotional pressure finding expression
- **Reflection** — memories and beliefs bubbling up during sleep

A dream fragment is 3–8 words: `"rain, waiting, closed door"` or `"phone light, unanswered message"`. It is a snapshot of psychological state, not a plot.

The system knows *what kind* of dream the character is having (tone, intensity, clarity) and *what it's about* (symbols from memories, needs, beliefs). It does not generate paragraphs of dream narrative.

## Architecture

### DreamTone (8 tones)

```
calm | anxious | lonely | warm | fragmented | unclear | hopeful | threatening
```

Tone is selected based on context (stress, loneliness, quality, fatigue), with seeded RNG for tie-breaking among equally weighted candidates.

### DreamSource

Dream content originates from four source types:

| Kind | Weighted by | Emotional charge |
|---|---|---|
| `memory` | importance (60%) + recency (40%) | importance (70%) + recency (30%) |
| `belief` | strength | strength × 0.5 |
| `need` | intensity | intensity |
| `cluster` | mass (70%) + instability (30%) | mass |

`selectDreamSources` is purely deterministic — sorts by weight descending, takes top N.

### DreamContext

| Field | Source | Role |
|---|---|---|
| `sleepPhase` | SleepWakeState | Awake/waking → no dream; deep_sleep → strong dream |
| `sleepQuality` | SleepWakeState | Higher → calmer/clearer dreams |
| `stressLoad` | Boundary | High → anxious/threatening |
| `fatigue` | EnergyFatigueState | High → fragmented/unclear |
| `loneliness` | MetaState (inverted tolerance) | High → lonely tone |
| `activeMemoryCount` | Memories.length | Indicator of source material |
| `seed` | Required | Deterministic generation |

### DreamFragment

```typescript
{
  id, tone, intensity, clarity,
  sourceIds, symbols[], description,
  generated: true | false
}
```

- `generated=false` when no sources, awake phase, or chance too low in transitional phase
- `description` is a template-filled short fragment (≤12 words)
- `symbols` extracted from source labels via keyword extraction
- `clarity` = sleepQuality × 0.6 − stressLoad × 0.25 − fatigue × 0.25 + 0.3

### Tone Selection Model

Each of the 8 tones starts with a small base weight. Context modifiers adjust weights:

- **High stress** → anxious +35%, threatening +30%, fragmented +15%, calm −10%, warm −10%
- **High loneliness** → lonely +40%, warm −10%, hopeful −10%
- **High sleep quality** → calm +20%, warm +15%, hopeful +10%, fragmented −10%, unclear −10%
- **High fatigue** → fragmented +30%, unclear +25%, calm −10%, warm −5%
- **High avg emotional charge** → anxious +15%, fragmented +10%, calm −10%

Final tone selected via weighted random using SeededRandom.

### Description Generation

Template-based: 10 patterns matched by tone, filled with extracted symbols.

Example patterns:
- `["{symbol}", "waiting", "{symbol}"]` — lonely, unclear, fragmented
- `["warm light", "{symbol}"]` — warm, hopeful, calm
- `["rain", "{symbol}", "{symbol}"]` — calm, lonely, fragmented

## Generation Flow

1. **Build DreamContext** from character state (read-only)
2. **Select DreamSources** from memories, beliefs, needs, clusters
3. **Phase check**: awake/waking → no dream; drowsy → low chance; sleep → active
4. **Determine tone** from context-weighted selection with seeded RNG
5. **Compute intensity/clarity** from context
6. **Extract symbols** from source labels (keyword extraction)
7. **Build description** from tone-matched template + symbols
8. **Return DreamTrace** with fragment, sources, reasons, warnings

## Design Philosophy

> Dreams are not story generation. Dreams are the residue of memory, lack, stress, and desire surfacing during sleep.

CharacterOS should not let AI "write dreams." CharacterOS should let the character's experiences float up during sleep.

A dream fragment is a closed door, a distant voice, rain on a window — not a plot with a beginning, middle, and end. The meaning belongs to the character, not the narrator.

**Status:** ✓ Complete

## Objective

Bridge the V10 Continuous Life system (V10.1–V10.10) with the V10.12–V10.14 Differentiated Decision Pipeline. Life signals from a dry-run life tick now act as **modifiers** on personality-driven decisions — not primary drivers, but contextual influences on strategy weighting.

A character who is tired makes different choices than one who is restless, bored, or inspired.

---

## Why V10.15 Now

Before V10.15:

- `runLifeTickDryRun` tells us the character's internal state (energy, fatigue, boredom, thoughts, candidates)
- `buildDifferentiatedDecision` tells us what the character would do based on personality and environment
- But they didn't talk to each other

After V10.15:

- Life state **modifies** decision strategy weighting
- A tired character's strategies tilt toward safety and delay
- A restless character's strategies tilt toward exploration and trial
- A worried character's strategies tilt toward verification
- Personality schemas still dominate — life signals are modifiers, never overrides

## Life Modifier Rules

Life signals adjust strategy intensities via multiplicative modifiers (never less than 0.5×):

| Life Signal | Boosted Strategies | Suppressed Strategies |
|---|---|---|
| `fatigue > 0.55` or `sleepPressure > 0.55` | `delay_decision`, `resource_protective_refusal`, `emotional_withdrawal` (+8%) | `impulsive_join`, `direct_join`, `confront_directly`, `power_grab` (−10%) |
| `boredom > 0.4` or `restlessness > 0.4` | `small_scale_trial`, `reframe_as_growth`, `exploit_opportunity`, `freedom_preserving_choice` (+6%) | — |
| `strongestInspirationStrength > 0.4` | `reframe_as_growth`, `small_scale_trial` (+6%) | — |
| `irritability > 0.45` | `fairness_correction`, `confront_directly`, `revenge_or_rectify` (+5%) | — |
| `thought = "worry"` | `verify_before_commitment`, `seek_support` (+6%) | — |
| `thought = "memory_echo"` | `reframe_as_growth`, `attachment_checking` (+4%) | — |
| `sleepPhase = deep/light sleep` | — | All non-rest strategies (−15%) |

All modifications are recorded in `DifferentiatedDecision.lifeInfluences[]`.

## API Integration

### Default (backward compatible)

```
GET /api/characters/[characterId]/decision
→ same response as V10.12/V10.13
→ no life context
```

### With Life Context

```
GET /api/characters/[characterId]/decision?includeLifeContext=true
→ runs life tick dry-run (6h default, deterministic seed)
→ builds LifeDecisionContext from dry-run
→ re-derives differentiated decision with life modifiers
→ response includes:
   - lifeDecisionContext    (full LifeDecisionContext)
   - lifeInfluences         (string[] from differentiation engine)
   - differentiatedExplanation includes life context facts/reasons
```

Query params:
- `includeLifeContext=true|false`
- `elapsedHours` (default 6, clamped 1–168)
- `seed` (default `${characterId}-v10.15`)

### Safety Guarantees

- Dry-run only — never writes to `CharacterPhysicsState`
- Self-action candidates are **never executed**
- Legacy `BehaviorDecision` is unchanged
- Without `includeLifeContext`, response is identical to V10.13

## Known Limitations

- Life context only affects strategy selection, not schema activation — personality remains the primary driver of schema choice
- `elapsedHours` defaults to 6 — caller must provide if longer/shorter intervals are desired
- Life dry-run starts from default subsystem states each time — no persistent life state across API calls
- API-level re-derivation of differentiatedDecision means two calls can differ if state changes between them
